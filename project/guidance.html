Questions and Answers

Click a question below to expand the answer

1. How do I know what middle school my child will attend?

We coordinate with the middle schools during the transition from fifth to sixth grades. Our students are zoned for three different middle schools (Rock Lake, Teague or Milwee) depending upon the home address. Students may also attend magnet schools through an application process. Please contact the Guidance Department for more information on the Magnet Programs or middle schools. To find out what school your address is zoned for please visit http://www.scps.k12.fl.us/ and type in your address under school zone search. If you have any questions about your middle school zoning please contact the Choices department http://www.scps.k12.fl.us/choices/index.cfm or call them at 407-320-0329.

2. What kind of training do school counselors have?

School counselors have a minimum of a Master's degree with state certification in Guidance and Counseling.

3. What is a school counselor?

"Today's school counselors are vital members of the education team. They help all students in the areas of academic achievement, personal/social development and career development, ensuring today's students become the productive, well-adjusted adults of tomorrow" (ASCA, 2007).

4. Why school counselors at the elementary level?

“The elementary years are a time when students begin to develop their academic self-concept and their feelings of competence and confidence as learners. They are beginning to develop decision-making, communication and life skills, as well as character values. It is also a time when students develop and acquire attitudes toward school self, peers, social groups and family. Comprehensive developmental school counseling programs provide education, prevention and intervention services, which are integrated into all aspects of children’s lives. Early identification and intervention of children’s academic and personal/social needs is essential in removing barriers to learning and in promoting academic achievement. The knowledge, attitudes and skills that students acquire in the areas of academic, career and personal/social development during these elementary years serve as the foundation for future success” (ASCA, 2007).

5. How does guidance support academic skills?

With... test interpretation, advisement, study skills, organization, test taking skills, academic and behavioral goal setting, school transitions, and career exploration.

6. How does guidance support personal and social skills?

With... violence prevention, anger management, groups for grief and loss, divorce and changing families, mentoring, and making and keeping friends.

7. What are some community resources available to my child?

The Grove, Intervention Services Inc., Safehouse of Seminole, Children's Home Society, Boys and Girls Town of Central FL, Behavior Support Services, Inc., A Better Therapy, Inc., Quest Kids, Open Door Social Services, Informed Families, Hospice of the Comforter, and Choices-Changes, Inc.

8. What are the screenings for Gifted services and who can request a screening?

Guidance will do the initial IQ screening that helps determine whether or not a child may be eligible for gifted services. If the child meets the state criteria, then the child is referred to the school psychologist for further testing. Either the teacher or parent can request a Gifted screening. We always need parent permission to screen. Once we receive a request for screening guidance will have a form for the parent to sign so we can complete necessary screenings.

9. What do I do if I need help with uniforms?

Spring Lake has a mandatory uniform policy. If you are in need of assistance in this area please call, Alison Johnson or Meg Anerson, Guidance Counselor at 407-746-1604/407-746-1649.

10. Does the school offer group and individual counseling?

Changing Families, Social Skills, Anger Management, Self-esteem, Friendship Skills, and Study Skills are a few of the counseling groups we offer at Spring Lake. Groups are formed on an as needed basis. Not all groups are offered at all times. If you are interested in your child joining a group please let the classroom teacher or guidance know.

11. What is a behavior plan?

Some students are placed on behavior plans through the Guidance Department. These programs are designed to promote positive behaviors and to assist students with behavior modifications.

12. What is the attendance policy?

Please visit our Policy Page

13. Does my child receive a hearing or vision screening?

Certain grades have mass screenings throughout the year. If a need for a vision or hearing screening arises it will be scheduled through the Guidance Department. If a student is identified as having a concern from the screenings, they are re screened and parents are notified.

14. What is Red Ribbon Week and Violence Prevention Week?

Red Ribbon Week is a nationwide effort to discourage drug use. Spring lake participates in various activities including "Wear Red Day" as a symbol for all to be Drug Free.
Violence Prevention Month is a school wide effort to reduce violence in schools. There are numerous activities throughout the month to help students make the commitment to be violence free.

15. What is the Home/Hospital Program?

Any public or private school student whose physician or psychiatrist certifies that the student: is not able to attend regular school, is able to participate in the benefit from Home/Hospital instruction, does not have a communicable disease, and is expected to be out of school three (3) weeks or longer, or has a chronic illness requiring extended absences throughout the school year. Application forms can be obtained through the Guidance Department.

16. Are Guidance Counselor's available for parent conferences?

Yes, either the teacher or the parent can request that a Guidance Counselor be present during a meeting.

17. What is Multi-Tiered System of Support MTSS?

MTSS is a coherent continuum of evidence based, system-wide practices to support a rapid response to academic and behavioral needs, with frequent data-based monitoring for instructional decision-making to empower each Florida student to achieve high standards.
